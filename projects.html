Considerations

Mobile Friendly

Since consumers are overwhelmingly accessing the internet via mobile devices, a good web strategy makes use of a well-designed mobile site as well as a fully-featured desktop version.

Form follows function

Even on a desktop, consumers primarily need information when they visit a website. What do they see first? What parts are 'above the fold' and which are below? Does the navigation bar make sense? I try to simplify the route to getting relevant information as much as possible - it should not take more than three clicks to get what you need.

Custom vs out of the box?

What's better, a templated site or a bespoke solution? It depends on the needs of the customer. Generic solutions can often be quickly customized, satisfying a customer's budgetary needs as well as maintaining individuality.

Web Apps

RecipeBox

This project was an opportunity to learn node, Express, and basic routing.

I designed the app to allow users to maintain a personal recipe box, from which they could share recipes or keep them private. Users login via local authentication or Twitter.

The user can also view a stream of recent recipes made by other people on the service, comment on recipes and/or pictures, and Tweet/Facebook their own creations.
